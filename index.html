Executive Summary

**WASTEPRENEURS CHAD** is a socially-driven waste management startup based in N’Djamena, Chad, dedicated to tackling the urgent environmental and socioeconomic challenges facing the country. With improper waste disposal contributing to severe air, water, and soil pollution, as well as significant health risks to local communities, WASTEPRENEURS CHAD provides an innovative, community-centered solution that combines environmental sustainability with economic empowerment for disadvantaged youth and women.

Chad faces a mounting waste crisis: over 70% of household and industrial waste is improperly managed, leading to polluted rivers, contaminated drinking water, and rising respiratory illnesses among children and adults. Simultaneously, youth unemployment in Chad exceeds 50%, and women are disproportionately excluded from formal economic opportunities. By addressing these intertwined issues, WASTEPRENEURS CHAD not only reduces environmental hazards but also empowers marginalized populations through dignified, sustainable work.

The startup offers localized waste collection, recycling education, training programs, and market development for recycled materials. In addition, it is pioneering a blockchain-based payment system to ensure transparency and fair compensation for community workers. The startup seeks **$250,000** through donation-based crowdfunding to finance two waste collection trucks, recycling equipment, workforce training, digital platforms for app-based waste pickup, and community engagement initiatives. The campaign will run for 45 days on platforms like GoFundMe or GlobalGiving, targeting environmentally-conscious donors, philanthropists, and impact investors worldwide. Through storytelling, emotional engagement, and robust social media outreach, WASTEPRENEURS CHAD aims to raise both funds and awareness, creating a ripple effect that promotes sustainable development, environmental responsibility, and socioeconomic empowerment in Chad. Donors will directly contribute to a cleaner environment and meaningful livelihood opportunities for women and youth.

Vision & Mission

WASTEPRENEURS CHAD’s **vision** is a pollution-free Chad where every community member has the opportunity to thrive through sustainable economic activity. Our **mission** is to transform the waste landscape of N’Djamena by providing accessible, reliable, and ethical waste management services that empower marginalized youth and women.

Solutions Offered

  • **Community-Based Waste Collection and Recycling:** We establish local collection points and use small, locally-managed teams (staffed primarily by youth and women) for residential and commercial waste pickup.
  • **Economic Empowerment and Skill Development:** We run certified training programs in sorting, processing, upcycling, and waste management entrepreneurship.
  • **Tech-Enabled Transparency and Market Access:** We utilize a proprietary mobile application for streamlined pick-up scheduling and a blockchain-based ledger to ensure transparent, timely payment and market access for recycled commodities.

Crowdfunding Campaign: The Impact Goal

Our immediate crowdfunding effort aims to raise **$250,000** over a 45-day campaign. This critical funding will be allocated directly to purchasing essential infrastructure: two specialized waste collection trucks, initial recycling processing equipment, and launching our intensive training program for the first cohort of 50 Wastepreneurs (youth and women).

Achieving this goal will enable us to start operations in three key neighborhoods of N’Djamena, removing over **500 tons of waste per month** and generating immediate, dignified employment where it is needed most. Every dollar contributed is a direct investment in a cleaner, more prosperous future for Chad's communities.

The 45-Day Campaign Plan: Strategy & Outreach

The Campaign Plan is structured around maximizing visibility and donor engagement across multiple channels:

  • **Phase 1 (The Launch):** A strong media partnership announcement and an emotional video story, driving traffic to the main campaign platform (e.g., GlobalGiving).
  • **Phase 2 (Mid-Campaign Momentum):** Targeted social media advertising focusing on the social and economic impact, weekly progress updates, and a major virtual community event.
  • **Phase 3 (Final Push):** Personalized outreach to impact investors and high-net-worth individuals, using detailed impact reports (e.g., "$100 buys 10 recycling bins"). We will offer tiered recognition, including virtual tours of N'Djamena's progress and official recognition on our new mobile app platform.

Execution Timeline: From Funding to Impact

Upon successful completion of the 45-day crowdfunding campaign, our operational timeline focuses on swift deployment and impact generation:

  1. **Month 1-2 (Procurement & Setup):**
    • Finalize purchase and logistics for 2 Waste Collection Trucks.
    • Acquire and install initial Recycling Processing Equipment.
    • Secure and establish 15 community collection points across the 3 pilot neighborhoods.
  2. **Month 3 (Launch Phase):**
    • Launch the intensive **Wastepreneur Training Program** for the first cohort (50 trainees).
    • Soft launch of the Mobile App (Phase 1) for worker tracking and payment ledger.
  3. **Month 4-6 (Initial Operations & Outreach):**
    • Begin full waste collection and processing operations in the 3 pilot neighborhoods.
    • Launch the city-wide Community Recycling Education Campaigns.
    • Establish first bulk recycling sales contracts with local and regional buyers.
  4. **Month 7-12 (Growth & Evaluation):**
    • Expand operations into 2 new target neighborhoods, onboarding a second cohort of Wastepreneurs.
    • Conduct a full impact evaluation, analyzing tons of waste diverted and jobs created.
    • Prepare for next funding round based on proven operational model and results.
